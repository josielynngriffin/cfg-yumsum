CROISSIANTS

PREP TIME: 10 minutes | COOK TIME: 1 hour 30 minutes | TOTAL TIME: 1 hour 50 minutes

INGREDIENTS

  • 2¼ teaspoons (1 packet) instant yeast
  • ½ cup water, room temperature
  • 3½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup whole milk - I have also used 2% milk
  • 1 large egg, beaten - used to brush the croissants
  • 1½ cups (3 sticks) unsalted butter, cut into cubes - slightly cool, not completely at room temp
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

DIRECTIONS

Step 1

Remove the milk and butter from the refrigerator. Cut the butter into tablespoon cubes and set aside.

Step 2

Spray a large mixing bowl with cooking spray and set aside. This will be the bowl used to proof the dough.

Step 3

In the work bowl of the stand mixer, add 3 and 1/2 cups of flour. Add the salt and sugar on one side of the bowl. Stir the dry ingredients with a whisk or the dough hook (before attaching) to combine. Add yeast to the side of the bowl away from the salt and stir to combine.

Step 4

Remove the dough from the mixer and gently form into a smooth ball in your hands. Place in the prepared mixing bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Rest the dough in a warm place for at least 30 minutes.

Step 5

Wipe out the bowl of the mixer (doesn't have to be totally clean), and attach the paddle attachment to the mixer. Add the cubed butter and 2 tablespoons of flour and beat until smooth and creamy. Do not overmix – we are not mixing or creaming as we would for cookie dough.

Step 6

Once the dough has proofed, pour it onto a generously floured work surface, and sprinkle the top lightly with flour. Roll the dough into a square, approximately 10-inch x 10-inch. Let this cool in the fridge for an hour.

Step 7

Roll and fold the dough packet from left to right, folding in the sides with each turn. Do this for a total of three turns and refridgerate for each turn.

Step 8

Roll dough onto surface again and cut into six croissant triangles with a sharp knife. Starting with the wide end of the triangle, roll up to the point. Proof in the oven or in a warm place until doubled in size.

Step 9

Preheat the oven to 375F degrees and adjust the oven rack to the middle position. Brush the beaten egg on each croissant and bake for 20 minutes – rotating the pans halfway through – until the croissants are nice and golden. Immediately transfer the croissants to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy!
